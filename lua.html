Lua

Most high-level programming languages are fairly similar, in that, they have a way to "print" or "write" a string.
In Lua, there is a print() function / statement that prints whatever is within its parentheses to the application, console, etc. In the parentheses, the value can either be a string, variable, or integer. To put a string into the parentheses just add double quotes around the word(s) or letter(s) you would like to print | Ex. ("word"). To print out a variable just put the variable name in the parentheses without quotes around it | Ex. (variable). Lastly, to print out an integer just put the number between the parentheses without quotes around it | Ex. (10).

Simple Lua String Print Statement:print("Hello World")


Simple Lua Variable Print Statement:print(Variable)
